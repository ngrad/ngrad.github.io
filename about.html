About

Nikole is an interactive designer creating inspiring, emotional engagement on the web and beyond. She focuses on user experience fundamentals and blending them with usability philosophies, while making sure everything is cohesive and elegant. She believes beautiful, successful design is born from a sound and thoughtful process.

Nikole received her BFA in Graphic Design at Salisbury University in Maryland, where she worked with Redhead Companies and Merkle and received the faculty's President's Award for "San Francisco". She thrives off working with clients who are as passionate about their product as she is about design. With this perfect combination, true innovation and creative work can be developed.

Color Ninja of the DC Jewish Community Center, Nikole single-handedly took the center through a rebrand to a more vibrant and streamlined image in the community. To follow, she accepted the Design Director role at NAV, a design studio in Washington, DC, where she focused on user interface design and user exeperience for desktop and mobile. Nikole is curently serving on the design team at the Sunlight Foundation. The team is adopting innovative design research practices and creative front end development design.

Along with design, Nikole enjoys playing competitive ultimate frisbee, time with her family dog, Country, and baseball. She finds inspiration from the vibrancy of Washington, DC, and the one the only Georgia O'Keefe.

"So I said to myself, 'I have things in my head that are not like what anyone has taught me - shapes and ideas so near to me - so natural to my way of being and thinking that it hasn't occurred to me to put them down.' I decided to start anew - to strip away what I had been taught - to accept as true my own thinking. I was alone and singularly free, working into my own, unknown - no one to satisfy but myself."

-Georgia O'Keefe."
